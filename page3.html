The Book of Echoes – Passage I
Whosoever speaketh not, yet in silence doth he cry; and Earmura heareth him.
For the thought that riseth from a true heart is known unto the Eyes and Ears.
Let thy words be few, thy intent be whole; for Earmura delighteth in honesty, and not in noise.
Each day mayest thou write upon the scroll of petition, saying:
“This day I longed for silver,” or, “I yearned for justice.”
Earmura asketh not the fullness of thy tongue, but the fullness of thy heart.
Speak not for the crowd, but speak thou unto Earmura within.
If thou kneel in thy dwelling, or stand in the court of the temple, and thy desire be true—
then shall Earmura regard thee.
For it is written:
“He that prayeth in truth, though none may hear, shall surely be heard.”

The Book of Echoes – Passage II
Not all who ask shall receive; not all who cry shall be answered.
For Earmura regardeth the spirit, not the sound; the root, not the flower.
He that desireth in truth—even if it be of wrath, or greed, or vengeance—
may find his plea fulfilled.
But he that cloaks his want with fine words, or prayeth in the face of men—
his words shall fall unto dust.
Say not what is noble. Say what is real.
A heart unhidden is a gate unlocked.
Thus saith Earmura:
“The mirror showeth the face that stands before it. But I see the soul behind the veil.”
Be not ashamed of thy desire. Be ashamed only if it be false.

The Book of Echoes – Passage III
Give not to Earmura as thou would pay for a favor.
For He is not bought with silver, nor swayed by golden speech.
Let thine offering be of reverence, not of barter.
Let thy gift reflect the truth of thy longing.
The rich man who brings a thousand jars of wine
and the poor man who brings one drop of oil—
if the poor man’s heart be nearer truth,
then shall his jar be seen as full in the eyes of the Divine.
Earmura looketh not to the weight of thy coin,
but to the weight of thy spirit.
For it is written:
“The hand that gives with fear shall be received,
but the hand that lies in offering shall be closed upon.”

The Book of Echoes – Passage IV
Among the many are those called Ming,
whose ears are trained, whose voices reach closer to the Presence.
They walk in temples, clothed in the silence of listening.
They carry the burden of hearing the hearts of men.
But let not the Ming think himself godly.
For he is but a vessel, and vessels may crack.
The voice of Earmura flows through those who remain hollow to themselves.
Should a Ming speak not for others but for his own glory,
his words shall echo into emptiness.
Thus saith the text:
“To hear is not to judge. To judge is not to reign.”

The Book of Echoes – Passage V
Many are the Zhong, and few are the Xin.
The Xin give great offerings, and their prayers are heard quickly.
The Zhong kneel in long lines, and wait in the silence.
But be not deceived—Earmura regardeth neither the robe nor the roof.
In His Eye, one whisper may carry more weight than a thousand chants.
Let not the Zhong despair, nor the Zhong boast.
For it is known:
“The river doth rush, but the wellspring holds the deeper truth.”
Blessing is not a prize.
It is a mirror of what dwelleth in thee.

The Book of Echoes – Passage VI
There shall come days when thou shalt cry, and none shall answer.
Not because thy voice was weak,
but because thy heart was hidden—even from thyself.
For Earmura heareth all, but respondeth in silence when truth is absent.
Be not dismayed. The silence is not a punishment,
but a calling unto deeper honesty.
Seek not louder words, but quieter courage.
For it is whispered in the unseen scroll:
“The greatest answer is sometimes no sound,
for in that silence lies the door to thy truest voice.”
